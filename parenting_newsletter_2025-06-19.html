How to Handle Our Kids’ Obsessive Jealousy

Janet Lansbury – 2025-06-08

In her article, Janet Lansbury suggests ways to handle our youngsters' seemingly relentless jealousy. She describes familiar situations, such as inappropriate toy snatching, and highlights that losing our cool is not the answer. Instead, she recommends calmly acknowledging a child's feelings, but not indulging the jealousy itself. Scenario play and setting suitable boundaries can be beneficial. She also suggests pausing before intervening in sibling disputes, providing the children with time to develop their negotiation skills. This approach helps children better understand their feelings and become more self-reliant, thereby leading to peaceful resolutions.

Struggling with a Strong-Willed Toddler’s Tantrums

Janet Lansbury – 2025-06-08

Chin up, parents! Janet Lansbury shares insights on managing spirited toddlers' tantrums, crucial for those blessed with feisty little ones. A mum of a two-and-a-half-year-old is concerned about her darling's aggressive behaviours– getting physical and raising the decibels when denied her desires. Janet's advice? Firstly, keep calm – your composed manner will help soothe their heated flares. Next, affirm their feelings. It's alright for them to express disagreement and frustration. Lastly, assert boundaries and don't shy away from saying "no". It's not always easy, but this approach can make transitions to school smoother. Remember, it's all learning and growing - for both of you.
