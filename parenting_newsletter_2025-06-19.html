This mom’s ruler trick at a party just taught a social lesson everyone needs

Motherly – 2025-06-17

Encountering new friends can be overwhelming for our little ones, this Mum found a way through. Using a ruler as a prop, she cleverly navigated a roleplay scenario teaching her child how to introduce unfamiliar friends. The 'ruler introduction', provides a physical embodiment of connections and spaces. One hand represents one friend, the other, a new acquaintance, and the ruler signifies the bridge between them. This seemingly simple tactic fosters social skills, encourages empathy, and could notably ease the challenging transition to preschool or reception. Definitely a hack worth trying, making your little one the politest, most considerate individual at their next gathering.

Discipline Isn’t Working – 3 Common Reasons and What To Do Instead

Janet Lansbury – 2025-06-08

Even for the most patient parents, disciplining toddlers can often seem like an uphill battle. Janet Lansbury suggests we might unknowingly be making three common errors: we might be trying to stop behaviour without understanding its root cause; we're setting boundaries but not ensuring they're comprehensible or enforceable; or we're losing our cool when things don't go to plan. Instead, she advises seeking to comprehend why the behaviour is happening, making sure our set boundaries are comprehensible and achievable, and quite crucially, maintaining our own calm. Be patient, love—parenting is a marathon, not a sprint!
